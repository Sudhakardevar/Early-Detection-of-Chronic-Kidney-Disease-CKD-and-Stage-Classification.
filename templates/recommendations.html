Chronic Kidney Disease(CKD) Prevention Steps

Based on the data you entered, here are some personalized health recommendations:

Diet and Nutrition

  • Control blood pressure by limiting salt intake and avoiding high-sodium foods.
  • Eat a balanced diet with moderate protein and low potassium foods.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated and maintain kidney function.
  • Avoid high-sodium and sugary processed foods to prevent complications.
  • Increase fiber intake by eating fruits, vegetables, and whole grains to maintain overall health.

Blood Pressure Management

  • Monitor blood pressure regularly (target below 130/80 mm Hg) to reduce the risk of kidney damage.
  • Take prescribed medications consistently, as directed by your healthcare provider.
  • Exercise regularly to maintain a healthy blood pressure and improve heart health.
  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption and smoking to prevent further health issues.

Diabetes Management

  • Keep blood glucose levels under control by following prescribed treatment plans.
  • Follow a low-sugar, high-fiber diet to manage blood sugar levels effectively.
  • Exercise regularly to maintain a healthy weight and improve insulin sensitivity.
  • Avoid sugary snacks and drinks that can spike blood sugar levels.

Heart and Edema Health

  • Monitor for signs of coronary artery disease, such as chest pain or shortness of breath.
  • Keep weight under control to support heart and kidney function.
  • Watch for swelling (edema) and report it to your doctor immediately, as it could indicate fluid retention or heart problems.

Urinary and Infection Prevention

  • Stay hydrated to help prevent infections and maintain kidney function.
  • Maintain good hygiene, especially around the urinary tract, and avoid holding urine for long periods to prevent urinary tract infections (UTIs).
